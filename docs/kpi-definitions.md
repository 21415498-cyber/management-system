# KPI Definitions

## Introduction  
Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are essential metrics used to monitor the success of an organization in achieving its objectives. This document outlines the components of a KPI definition system.

## Types of KPIs  
1. **Quantitative KPIs**: Measurable and expressed in numbers (e.g., revenue, number of customers).  
2. **Qualitative KPIs**: Based on subjective judgment or opinion (e.g., customer satisfaction).  
3. **Leading KPIs**: Predict future performance (e.g., new leads, prospect inquiries).  
4. **Lagging KPIs**: Reflect past performance (e.g., total sales, profit margins).

## Calculation Methods  
- **Sum**: Total of a metric (e.g., total revenue = sum of monthly revenues).
- **Average**: Mean value (e.g., average customer satisfaction score).
- **Percentage**: Ratio expressed as a percent (e.g., conversion rate = (number of sales / number of leads) * 100).
- **Index**: Composite scores combining multiple metrics (e.g., customer loyalty index).

## Monitoring Frequency  
- **Real-time**: Continuous monitoring for immediate insights.
- **Daily**: Metrics updated at the end of each day.
- **Weekly**: Aggregated metrics reviewed weekly for performance trends.
- **Monthly**: In-depth review of metrics every month.
- **Quarterly**: Detailed analysis and adjustments every quarter.

## Target Setting  
- **SMART Criteria**: Targets should be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.
- **Historical Performance**: Use past data to inform realistic targets.
- **Benchmarking**: Compare against industry standards or competitors.
- **Stakeholder Input**: Involve relevant parties in target setting for better alignment.

## Data Sources  
- **Internal Systems**: ERP, CRM, and other databases.
- **Surveys and Feedback**: Direct input from customers and employees.
- **Market Research**: External data to inform benchmarks and targets.
- **Web Analytics**: Metrics from digital platforms and online engagement.

## Dashboard Metrics  
- **Traffic Metrics**: Number of visits and unique users.
- **Conversion Metrics**: Rates of conversion for various stages.
- **Customer Metrics**: Total revenue, average order value, customer lifetime value.
- **Operational Metrics**: Efficiency, capacity, and productivity measures.

## Conclusion  
Properly defined KPIs are critical for assessing the success of strategies and driving performance forward. Regular review and updates of KPIs will lead to more effective management and improvement in outcomes.
